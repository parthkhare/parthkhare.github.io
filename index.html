Parth Khare

I am a Data Science Consultant to the Chief Economic Adviser of India, working on exploring big data (satellite, nightlights with high frequency socio-economic data) solutions for guiding economic policies in India. My work on subjectively tuned machine learning parameters on satellite imagery for improving property tax collection by more than 80% has been featured in Economic Survey of India, 2016-17 . Recently, I have worked on first big data project (~120 million obervations) undertaken by the government assessing performance of biometric linked social sector schemes, inlcuding MNREGA, PDS and Pension. In the Volume II Economic Survey 2016-17, I had proposed for a revision the definition of Urbanization in India by integrating dynamically updated Satellite based built-up & population data to the Census based definition (Pg 221-). The shared enterprises have affected considerable policy impact by realising improved source financing city revenue , as well as unique in terms of formalising use of big data and satellite imagery in government policy making

For the last 5 years, I have worked extensively on implementing parametric and non-parametric statistical methods on Insurance, Consumer, Public Census and Satellite Imagery data. I have conducted training sessions on R and Decision Trees to Indian Economic Service probationers, bureaucrats and in the private sector. Duing my term as Manager, Data Science at Nielsen, I was succsessful in coding a model architecture in R, which estimates socio-economic attributes at 1 square km level precision GRID. The algorithm, implemented on Asian and European geographies works by interacting public census data with micro satellite imagery data to unearth spatially intelligent patterns. The hidden potential of layering multiple data sources had motivated me on developing open source solution to aid public policy. Some interactive map applications on Indian data: India Crime Analysis, Delhi Crime Deployment, R Shiny Maps & Voronoi Maps.

I have developed several customized geo-spatial functions in R to ease spatial geometry processing in R. In addition I have created an optimsiation function, GBM optimizor to help in choosing optimal parameters values(trees, shrinkage, OOB etc) for targeted identifcation of local & global minima. Learning R for the first time can get involving due to multiple syntaxes for same operation, R Utility Kit is an attempt to streamline codes on basic data operation, classified by commonly used processes string manipulations, merging/reshaping etc.

With core specialization towards public data, I have worked extensively on improving improving quality and embellishing micro level variance in public data by leveraging cross intelligence from different data sets primarily satellite imagery. Census and associated survey based data sets are inured with issues of biased sampling, incomplete coverage as well as information authenticity. Moreover, such information is also limited by it's density and the the identified proxies to such data also fail to capture a respectable variance. Satellite data in this respect addresses most of these issues by providing bais free, exhaustive information on land use, building/road structures as well as location of commercial points such as ATM's, Gas Stations and finally serving as universal merging ID to collate disparate data sets toghether.
